My name is Lance Malcolm Giles and I am an artist.


I write, make music, make guitar effects and other electronic and digital devices, and do stuff with computers.

I include many things that have happened to me in my fiction writing. Because they are hard for me to conceptualize otherwise. Life is a metaphor. In a similar spirit, I enjoy writing about various topics I find interesting as I find writing a useful way to organize and develop my own thoughts on a topic.

I make primarily instrumental experimental music. I have influences in many places, but at the end of the day, I find words limiting and often find myself best able to express myself with a guitar. If it were easy to say what it all meant, I probably wouldn’t need to express it as music.

I approach electronics and programming in the same way that I approach writing and music--they are creative outlets, just of a different nature.

The thing about guitar pedals and the like is that no one really uses them the same way because no one has the exact same equipment, temperament, and everything else the contributes to tone. So the guitar pedals I make have a more general appeal than, say, the music I make with them. Everyone has different equipment and ideas and thus anyone who is interested in experimenting--from trying out new textures of distortion to match some sound in mind or on a record to using novel noisemaking devices, my devices will appeal.

I first became interested in programming for the sake of making games and worlds, but after some experimenting, I found myself much more interested in creating tools for music. There’s something very appealing about the fact that with some knowledge and a text editor, there lies an infinity of potential things to build.

I don’t talk about it much, but various spiritual experiences I had in my youth color the way I see this world to this day, and being one who creates is an extension of that. The list of things I want to make is neverending. This is why I find some comfort with the label of an artist.

My work is likely of interest to those interested in the occult, chaos, improvisation, surrealism, and psychedelia.

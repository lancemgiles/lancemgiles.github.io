DESIGN

From pedal graphics to T-shirts to book covers, I try to constrain my designs by using free and open source tools such as Inkscape and GIMP.

While this approach might not be the most common among graphic designers, the only stumbling blocks I have came across from this workflow have more to do with working with other companies rather than technical limitations of these programs.

The most poigent example of this is that to print my effect pedal designs in light colors, my printing company of choice requires a specific, Adobe branded white to be placed underneath the color. My way around has been to adopt stipling as my primary graphic style, allowing me to print monochrome designs which still have depth and shadow.

In addition to graphic design, I am very experienced in more traditional art forms. Many of my guitar pedals are hand-painted or hand-engraved.
